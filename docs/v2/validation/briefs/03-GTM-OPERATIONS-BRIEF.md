# Research Brief: GTM Operations Agent

**Agent ID**: GTM Operations Agent
**Phase**: Phase 3 (Day 3-4)
**Priority**: P1 - Important

---

## Mission

Define the operational mechanics of HOW OpenVibe acquires customers through partner-led and direct channels.

**Output**: Customer acquisition playbook, sales process, marketing strategy, content roadmap

---

## Context

**What we know**:
- GTM is partner-led (120 partners → 11,500 customers in 18 months)
- 4 partner verticals: Consulting → Accounting → MSPs → Marketing agencies
- Pricing: $149/mo/board, 90-day free trial
- Alpha (Month 1-2), Beta (Month 3-5), GA (Month 6)

**What we DON'T know** (your job):
1. How do we acquire partners? (Specific tactics)
2. How do partners sell to end customers? (Sales playbook)
3. What's the sales cycle for each stage?
4. What's the marketing strategy to support this?
5. What content is needed at each stage?

**Dependencies**:
- Customer Intelligence Agent: ICP, decision chain, use cases
- Unit Economics Agent: CAC targets, budget constraints

---

## Research Objectives

### 1. Customer Acquisition Strategy

**Objective**: Define detailed acquisition tactics for partner + direct channels

**What to define**:

**Partner Acquisition (Primary Channel)**:

**Lead Generation**:
- How do we identify target partners? (Database? Industry lists?)
- What's the outreach strategy? (Cold email? Warm intro? Events?)
- What's the value prop to partners? (Revenue opportunity, client value)

**Partner Onboarding**:
- What does partner onboarding look like? (Training? Certification?)
- How long does it take?
- What support do they need?

**Partner Enablement**:
- What materials do partners need? (Deck? Demo? Case studies?)
- How do we train partners to sell?
- What ongoing support is provided?

**Research sources**:
- HubSpot partner program case study
- GitLab channel partner playbook
- Zapier partner strategy
- B2B2B GTM best practices

**Direct Acquisition (Secondary Channel)**:

**Channels**:
- Paid (Google, LinkedIn, industry pubs)
- Organic (SEO, content marketing)
- Community (open source community, forums)
- Events (conferences, webinars)

**Funnel**:
- Awareness → website visit
- Consideration → sign up for trial
- Decision → convert to paid
- Conversion rates at each stage?

**Research sources**:
- B2B SaaS acquisition channel mix
- Open source → commercial funnel (GitLab, Sentry)
- AI product marketing case studies

**Deliverable**: Acquisition playbook with:
- Channel-by-channel tactics
- Lead generation strategies
- Qualification criteria
- Handoff processes

### 2. Sales Cycle & Process

**Objective**: Define timeline and steps from lead to close

**What to define**:

**Partner Sales Cycle**:

**Stage 1: Partner Lead → Qualified Partner**
- How do we qualify? (Criteria: client base, vertical fit, capacity)
- Who qualifies? (BDR? Partner team?)
- Timeline: X days

**Stage 2: Qualified Partner → Partner Sign-up**
- Demo/pitch to partner leadership
- Contract negotiation (wholesale pricing, terms)
- Onboarding kickoff
- Timeline: Y days

**Stage 3: Partner Onboarding → First Client Deployment**
- Training & certification
- Sales enablement
- First client pitch (co-sell)
- Timeline: Z days

**End Customer Sales Cycle** (via partner):

**Stage 1: Partner Recommends → Client Interest**
- Partner introduces OpenVibe to client
- Initial demo
- Timeline: X days

**Stage 2: Client Interest → Trial Start**
- Sign up process
- Onboarding
- Timeline: Y days

**Stage 3: Trial Start → Paid Conversion**
- 90-day trial period
- Usage during trial
- Conversion decision
- Timeline: 90 days

**Research sources**:
- Partner-led sales cycle benchmarks
- B2B SaaS sales cycle research (Gartner)
- Professional services buying cycles
- Trial-to-paid conversion timelines

**Deliverable**: Sales process flowcharts with:
- Stages clearly defined
- Timeline for each stage
- Stakeholders at each stage
- Success criteria for stage transitions
- Common failure points

### 3. Sales Process Definition

**Objective**: Step-by-step process for partner and end customer sales

**What to document**:

**Partner Sales Process**:
```
1. Lead generation (tactics)
2. Lead qualification (criteria, who does it)
3. Discovery call (what to ask)
4. Demo/pitch (deck, talking points)
5. Proposal (pricing, terms)
6. Negotiation (common objections, responses)
7. Contract (legal, procurement)
8. Onboarding (training, certification)
9. First deployment (co-sell support)
10. Ongoing enablement (QBRs, updates)
```

**End Customer Sales Process** (partner-led):
```
1. Partner identifies opportunity
2. Partner introduces OpenVibe
3. Discovery (pain points, use cases)
4. Demo (tailored to customer)
5. Trial (90 days)
6. Check-ins during trial
7. Conversion decision
8. Contract sign
9. Onboarding & training
10. Expansion (more boards)
```

**Research sources**:
- B2B SaaS sales process templates (Winning by Design)
- Channel sales best practices
- Professional services sales methodologies

**Deliverable**: Sales playbooks (2) with:
- Step-by-step process
- Talking points & scripts
- Objection handling
- Success metrics per stage

### 6. Discount & Pricing Policy

**Objective**: Define when and how to offer discounts

**What to define**:

**Standard Pricing**:
- $149/month/board (end customer)
- $79-99/month/board (partner wholesale)
- 90-day free trial (all boards at GA)

**Discount Scenarios**:

**Volume Discounts**:
- 10-50 boards: X% off
- 51-100 boards: Y% off
- 100+ boards: Z% off (enterprise pricing)

**Partner Discounts**:
- Early partner incentives (first 10 partners)
- Volume commitments (partner commits to X boards)

**Promotional Discounts**:
- Launch promotion (first 100 customers)
- Annual prepay (pay yearly, get 2 months free)

**Non-Discountable**:
- No discounts during 90-day free trial (already free)
- No discounts to direct customers below $149

**Research sources**:
- SaaS discount strategies
- Partner pricing models (wholesale vs revenue share)
- Volume discount best practices

**Deliverable**: Pricing policy document with:
- Standard pricing (by channel)
- Discount matrix (when, how much)
- Approval process (who can approve discounts)
- Discount constraints (what not to do)
